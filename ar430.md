# App Store Review Analysis (April 30)

During a review of the user flow and codebase for potential App Store review issues, the following items were identified that could lead to a rejection. 

## 1. Undocumented URL Scheme (`x-apple-health://`)
**Location:** `OnboardingView.swift` and `DashboardView.swift`
**Guideline:** 2.5.1 - Software Requirements (Public APIs)
**Issue:** The app attempts to open the Apple Health app directly using the `x-apple-health://` URL scheme. Apple considers this an undocumented, private URL scheme. Use of private APIs or undocumented schemes is a frequent cause for rejection.
**Potential Fix:**
Remove the `x-apple-health://` deep link. Instead, rely on `UIApplication.openSettingsURLString` to take the user to the app's iOS Settings page where they can manage Health access, or simply provide text instructions telling the user to open the Health app manually.

## 2. Missing Privacy Policy Link on Onboarding
**Location:** `OnboardingView.swift` (Phase: `.intro`)
**Guideline:** 5.1.1 (Data Collection and Storage) & 5.1.2 (Health Data)
**Issue:** The app requests HealthKit authorization when the user taps "CONNECT HEALTH". While there is a "PRIVATE & SECURE" panel explaining why the data is needed, Apple strictly requires that apps accessing sensitive data (like HealthKit) provide a clear, easily accessible link to the Privacy Policy *before* or *on the same screen* as the permission request. Currently, the Privacy Policy is only accessible deep within `SettingsView.swift`.
**Potential Fix:**
Add a `Link` or button that opens the Privacy Policy (`https://jackwallner.github.io/fitness-streaks/privacy-policy.html`) directly on the `introScreen` of `OnboardingView.swift`, ideally within or just below the privacy panel.

## 3. External Link for Virtual Coaching (Elsa Coach)
**Location:** `SettingsView.swift`
**Guideline:** 3.1.1 - In-App Purchase
**Issue:** The Settings view includes a prominent section promoting "Virtual personal training and nutrition coaching with Elsa," linking out to an external website (`https://www.e3fit.me/#services`). App Store Reviewers often flag external links for paid digital services as an attempt to bypass In-App Purchases (IAP). 
**Note / Potential Fix:** 
Under Guideline 3.1.1 ("1-to-1 experiences"), apps are allowed to use purchase methods other than IAP for real-time 1-to-1 experiences (like tutoring or fitness training). The app is technically compliant if the coaching consists of live, 1-to-1 virtual sessions. However, the reviewer might misunderstand the service. 
To prevent or prepare for this:
- Ensure the UI copy explicitly highlights that these are "1-on-1 live virtual sessions".
- In the App Store Connect review notes, proactively cite Guideline 3.1.1 "1-to-1 experiences" to explain the external link.
- If rejected, appeal with this specific exemption.

## 4. Notifications Authorization Timing
**Location:** `SettingsView.swift`
**Guideline:** 5.1.1 (i) Data Minimization
**Note:** The app correctly handles notification authorization by waiting until the user explicitly toggles "AT-RISK REMINDER" in the settings, rather than asking blindly on app launch. This is a great practice and complies perfectly with Apple's recommendations for user-initiated permission requests. No fix needed here, just noting it as a positive implementation detail that avoids a common rejection reason.
